How do I organize a cruise?
Contact Yacht and Go by phone or email. Our experts will help you prepare your special holiday. We will take into account the type of cruise you are looking for, the time you want to spend on board and the destinations and type of boat that are best for you. Together, with the information you give us, and our extensive knowledge of yachts and destinations, we will be able to prepare a formula for you.
When can I leave? How long in advance do I need to book?
Yacht and Go can arrange a charter for you next week or next year. However, if you want to book a particular boat or if your vacation dates are not flexible, it is best to do so as soon as possible.
What is the APA? How is it calculated?
Charter fees generally only cover yacht and crew rentals, while all other expenses are additional to charter fees. The needs and wishes of each tenant are different.
The APA-Advanced Provisioning Allowance is the standard way to pay expenses during your luxury cruise.
This is a simple and practical way to create a petty cash for your Captain who will use it to cover operational expenses, such as fuel consumed for the itinerary, food, wines, liqueurs, communication costs, shipping costs, transfers to/from the boat, special requests and incidental expenses. The Captain makes all payments from this account and is always obliged to keep all receipts and examine for you the balance sheet of the account. There is never a mark rate on the hull and you have access to the expense level at all times.
Any balance remaining at the end of your charter will be refunded in cash, and likewise, any excess costs will be refunded in cash to the Captain prior to your disembarkment. The Captain should warn you in advance if he notices that your expenses will exceed the cash on board so that there are no surprises.
The amount of the hull is estimated in advance and calculated as a percentage of the charter fee; in general, it is 20-25% for a sailboat and 30-35% for a motor boat, depending on the itinerary and the number of guests.
According to MYBA’s terms and conditions, the cabin cash is due at the time of the last payment of the charter fee. This allows the Captain and his crew to plan and personalize your stay on board prior to your arrival.
When do I have to make the final payment for my cruise?
Rental contracts may differ slightly for particular yachts around the world but in general, your balance and other related costs must be paid at least 30 days before the start of your cruise.

Can I be contacted while I’m away?
The majority of luxury yachts are equipped with satellite phones and Internet (there is sometimes a charge for this service) and most cruise destinations have mobile phone services available. In addition, we can provide the contact details of your cruise ship, for you to distribute them at your convenience to your family and friends before your departure.
What is the captain’s responsibility?
All yacht crews in the Yacht and Go catalog are professionally certified to ensure you the best safe cruising experience. The crews are also trained to provide you with high-quality hospitality and service, from dinner to relaxation and the safe use of water games equipment. The captain will consult you when necessary and will always try to accommodate your wishes . However, he will be responsible for the safety of the vessel and all passengers on board and will impose the final say when circumstances require it.
Do I give tips?
Even if it is at your discretion, it is customary to tip the captain and crew at the end of the cruise for good service. As an index, a 10-15% gratuity based on fees is considered fair while exceptional service and attention to detail may be worth a tip of 20% or more, but always according to your own good will.
How do I prepare my itinerary?
Yacht and Go does not make guesses when it comes to planning the itinerary of your private cruise. We will take your requirements into account down to the smallest detail, and together with your captain, we will develop a plan that will satisfy your desires. This includes your food and beverage preferences as well as the on-board and ground activities you want to enjoy and where and when you want to go, etc. Of course, once on board, you can change your itinerary as you wish, following your captain’s advice.
Can I choose the menu?
In a detailed questionnaire to be completed, you can of course inform us of your preferences regarding food and beverages as well as possible medical diets or requirements. This allows your crew to fully understand your requirements and provide you with the most appropriate service. All our yacht chefs are experienced and proud of the high-quality cuisine they present to their passengers.
