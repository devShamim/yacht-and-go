About South Pacific

La Côte d'Azur, or The French Riviera, is the piece of France's Mediterranean paradise that stretches from Cassis to the border with Italy. The Coast has always been identified with glamor, stars, sophistication and, of course, luxury yachts and yacht charters, attracting aficionados of pleasure, sun and fun for decades.

This picturesque coastline stretches along the Mediterranean Sea and encompasses such beautiful famous cities as Cannes, Monaco and Saint-Tropez being perfect stopovers for any luxury charter boat. But far beyond nightclubs, casinos, high-end boutiques, luxury hotels and Michelin-starred restaurants, the French Riviera exudes a special and authentic charm thanks to beach clubs, fishing villages, markets and locals living at their own pace.

A perfect area for luxury cruises, the French Riviera also promises secluded bays, quiet anchorages, marinas and many crystal clear waterways such as Porquerolles, the islands of Lérins and the bay of Agay, full of secluded anchorages.

Nice-Côte d'Azur Airport provides easy access to the Riviera and offers helicopter service to Monaco and Saint-Tropez for guests wishing to board or disembark while avoiding road traffic.

In fact, hosting the famous Monaco Yacht Show every September, the principality is the epicenter of luxury boats and a popular destination among sailing aficionados.

From Nice to Monaco

A classic region of the French Riviera is that between Nice and Monaco, a coast of breathtaking beauty and pretty cities like Villefranche-sur-Mer, Cap Ferrat, Roquebrune-Cap-Martin, Cap d'Ail and the medieval village of Èze.

The historic city center of Nice has recently been renovated to give it its former splendor. This lively city is considered by many as one not to be missed, especially for its galleries, shops and museums along the Promenade des Anglais, Old Nice and Castle Hill. For centuries the port of Nice has been protecting the city from potential invaders who might arrive by sea. The huge Opera House of Nice and all the hotels of the end of the century, such as the Hotel Negresco, always bear witness to the extravagance of the belle époque.

Villefranche-sur-Mer is a seaside resort bordering Nice overflowing with medieval remains in the form of pretty squares, cathedral and cobbled streets of the old town. The waterfront is filled with long sandy beaches, cafes and outdoor restaurants.

Èze

Only 2.5 km south of Monaco is the medieval village of Èze, a village perched with a rustic charm that offers incomparable views of the Mediterranean. This pedestrian village is dotted with old stone houses decorated with colorful planters, shaded squares, ancient fountains and relics of the past. The cliff side is also home to irresistible handicraft shops, a real treat of authenticity.

Monaco

Monuments, gardens, museums, marinas, stars, breathtaking landscapes and a lifestyle to be found nowhere else, Monaco undoubtedly deserves the title of Crown Jewel of the French Riviera.

Impossible to get bored in Monaco, there are always plenty of activities to do! Formula 1 takes place there every May and the best way to participate and follow her aboard a luxury boat docked in the port of Monaco close to the race circuit.

Monaco also hosts many other festivals such as exhibitions, festivals, trade fairs and product previews taking place at the Casino de Monte-Carlo, one of the world’s first major casinos, the Palace of Monaco, the Oceanographic Museum or on a balcony or terrace on the seafront.

The steep streets are lined with all the high-end boutiques: the climb will then be a great pleasure. The two ports hosting luxury yachts visiting Monaco are Port Hercules and Port de Fontvieille.

Renowned for its restaurants and nightlife, Monaco is a feast for lovers of gourmet meals to taste at the Café de Paris, Casino, Jimmy'z or Buddha Bar.

Menton is a pretty town perched always keeping a quiet and refined rhythm. The hottest and most Italian city on the French Riviera, Menton remains sunny for 316 days of the year and is known for its picturesque berries, lemon orchards and gardens of topiaries, labyrinths, fragrant roses and impressive palm trees.

Don’t forget to visit the mermaid and dolphin fountain in this historic coastal town hosting luxury yachts from around the world. Menton’s agenda is always filled with social, sports and cultural activities.

From Nice to Cannes

The French Riviera stretching from Nice to Cannes and dotted with such cities as Mougins, Biot, Cagnes, Vallauris and Villeneuve Loubet and is recognized as an enclave of great artists, sculptors, authors and philosophers of the 20th century, including Monet, Renoir, Picasso, Rodin, Lêger or Cocteau and a myriad of other legendary talents all gathered in these cities perched and inspired by the surroundings, the sea breezes and the maritime culture.

Saint-Paul-de-Vence

Two kilometers from Nice, this summit was once the favorite place of such influential artists as Picasso, Matisse and others. The village is still a popular spot among creative people these days. Known for its markets, galleries, walks and historical sites, Saint-Paul-de-Vence is probably the most visited city of the French Riviera and one of the most beautiful in Provence.

Antibes, Juan-les-Pins

Nestled between Nice and Cannes, Antibes keeps its old charm of medieval twin towers. It is surely one of the most adorable cities of the French Riviera preserving its traditions with pride, including its flower and fruit market. La Vieille Antibes is known for its cobbled streets keeping the ambiance of its artistic past in which names such as Picasso, Chagall, Matisse and others were local people.

Nearby is also Cap d'Antibes, one of the most chic and elegant places on the French Riviera, and the most relaxed Juan-les-Pins where, in the 1920s and 1930s, the great names of Scott and Zelda Fitzgerald, Rudolf Valentino, Charlie Chaplin, Douglas Fairbanks and other Americans solicited cultural and social entertainment. In fact, it is said that water skiing was invented here by these jet-setters of the 1920s.

The Cannes Film Festival welcomes more than 30,000 official visitors and 4,000 accredited journalists every year, but every year the city hosts even more fairs, events, conferences and festivals.

Renting a boat during the most important events is a great alternative to hotels in the city center. Yachts available for rent in Cannes are extremely popular and booked well in advance.

No matter what’s happening in Cannes, the city is ideal for strolling along the Boulevard de la Croisette, soaking up the surroundings from the seafront or enjoying a lunch in one of the beach-side restaurants coupled with spectacular views.

The islands of Lérins are a very small archipelago on the shores of Cannes, the two main islands of which are Sainte-Marguerite and Saint-Honorat. The first is the largest and known for the beautiful pine and eucalyptus forests lined with pedestrian paths running through the island. In the harbor are cafes and restaurants and a short walk away, the royal fort containing a cell where the legendary Iron Mask Man was incarcerated. Between the two islands, the Middle Plateau, a narrow passage and preferred anchorage for yachts.

Saint-Tropez has gained worldwide fame thanks to its luxury boutiques and trendy restaurants in the alleys that surround the city’s port. Famous since the 1950s when Brigitte Bardot enchanted the world with her bikini and French grace, the city of Saint-Tropez is always so charming and chic.

Filled with bistros, bars and ball fields, Saint-Tropez is a popular stopover during every luxury cruise. Among its many incarnations as a fishing village, a port, a military fortification and an enclave of artists, Saint-Tropez is a nice mix of everything for which the Côte d'Azur is so recognized and prized. The Place des Lices with markets on Tuesdays and Saturdays, the Citadelle, the Sainte-Anne Chapel and the Notre-Dame de l'Assomption are just a few of the places to visit in the gendarme’s city.

The islands of Hyères, or the Golden Islands, still south of the Coast, are a dream stop for guests looking for a peaceful place to swim, enjoy water sports or go cycling or hiking.
